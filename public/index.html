The Design Process

In general the design process is as follows: At the initial meeting Lee will look over the site, listen to your requirements and needs and take notes. He will e-mail you a proposal for your review. Upon approval of the proposal he will schedule an appointment to take measurements and a selection of photographs. Next the measurements are input to the computer to develop the base sheet, followed by the initial concept space treatments. When the plans are approximately 80% completed there is a meeting to review the work with an opportunity for review and input on the part of the client. Revisions are made as needed leading to the final meeting for presentation of the colored concept plan with notes. Other steps and processes will be followed on and as needed basis and will be spelled out in the initial proposal.

About Lee Harris

“Garden design has always interested me,” As a young child Lee first encountered a small pond in the backyard of his grandparents’ South Pasadena home. “This pond looked so natural yet purposeful, such a peaceful addition to a beautifully shaded patio and arbor. I was captivated by its trickling sound and the coolness of the water on such a warm summer day, surely it must have always been there as part of the land even before the house was built...” In reality the pond, patio, and arbor spaces were designed … by his mother’s cousin, well known Landscape Architect Courtland Paul, FASLA (1927-2003). A native Californian, who became one of the state’s first licensed landscape architects in 1954. Mr. Paul and Peridian International, the landscape architecture firm he founded over fifty years ago. “Were well known for community design and master planning, and for the golf courses, hotels, and theme parks they created in Southern California and throughout the world.”

Lee relies on his degree in Ornamental Horticulture and over 30 years of practical experience in landscape and swimming pool design. He has worked side by side with numerous contractors of all types. “I have a passion for creating spaces that move you literally and figuratively. A well-designed landscape should call to you. It should be something that becomes an extension of your life inside the home… it should draw you into the out-of-doors.”

Lee is also a 3 time participant of the Genesis 3 Swimming Pool Design School.

When not designing, Lee enjoys cooking and entertaining with his wife. His other creative pursuit is contemporary abstract oil painting. “Painting helps me see my surroundings in new ways. It keeps me fresh and provides another avenue for the creative process.”
